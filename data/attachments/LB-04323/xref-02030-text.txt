============

audiowhore repair project - 1976

source: LB-4323 (LK set: Low Gen / Master > CDR > EAC > FLAC)
(but see the notes about this confused LB entry below).


Notes about this recording:
- all thanks to the taper for being there
- mono recording
- the pitch and speed slid throughout the recording.
  For example, there are 3 x approximately 31 minute sections of the show where the speed/pitch progressively changed (either up or down) by about 1% to 1.5% during those sections
  within this, there were four songs where the sound changed by between 0.5% to 0.75% from the start to end of that song
  Blowin' In The Wind and Silver Mantis were more significantly off (see below, they have been patched in from an alt source).
- cuts or tape flips after tracks 1, 9, 10, 15, 16 and 17
- Blowin' In The Wind and the very start of Railroad Boy have been patched in from a poorer quality (but stereo!) source?
- Silver Mantis has been patched in from a much poorer quality source and has some tape damage and a minor cut in it
- tracks 11 and 19 are incomplete
- track 11 (Shelter From The Storm) is cut at 1:37 minutes
- track 19 (Idiot Wind) has the last 2 verses and chorus missed before returning for the ending (likely 2:30 minutes cut)
- nothing from this show has been officially released.


What i have done:
- pitch and speed correct each track individually - they varied throughout
- remove SBEs and other gaps
- eq mixing to better balance sound
- volume adjustments to balance left and right channels
- some hard limit on loud clapping
- tape flips, some static pops, microphone bumps cleaned/manually removed wherever possible
- No noise or hiss reduction used.


Notes about other versions of this show:
- LB-0566 (missing Blowin' In The Wind, Railroad Boy repeated)
- LB-3119 (differently processed version of LB-0566 but now includes Blowin' In The Wind)
- LB-4323 LK 1976 set with 2 songs patched from another source (this entry then refers to the JTT 2012 version from his lowgen tapes)
- LB-6723 is from the same tape as LB-4323 but pressed as a bootleg CD 'Highway 49 Revisited' (Top Cat label). Edited to fit on 1CD, 5 songs were removed
- LB-10010 shared by lowgen in 2012, same as LB-12058 but with some EQ (sourced from Mike D's original cassettes March 1977)
- LB-12058 shared by lowgen in 2015, same as LB-10010 but with no EQ (sourced from Mike D's original cassettes March 1977).

Note: The JTT version should have a separate LB entry and not be listed within LB-4323. They are from the same source but sound quite different.
