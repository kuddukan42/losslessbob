There are three LB numbers assigned to the Houston date:  LB-1430, LB-5845 (Legendary Taper C), & LB-9795 ("legendary taper who has an inital L"). jokerman.org speculates that the first 2 are from the same source, save that LB-5845 lacks the patch at the onset of the show, and & LB-9795 is from another. 

Comparing the 2 sources, jokerman.org suggests that "in comparison [the others have] a fuller warmer sound and [LB-9795] has more hiss but also more upfront sounding vocals."  

dolphinsmile posted LB-9795 in Dec. '11 and it has since fallen off the tracker.  I don't see that LB-1430 (or LB-5845) have been posted here since the EZT days.

So I am posting LB-1430 because it is more complete (it has the patch on the first tune).  But LB-1430 was a bit of a mess, with SBEs and extra RIFF chunks.  I fixed those (ref. shntool_len_original.txt, shntool_len_fixed).txt).  I converted the fixed files back to SHN and created a new md5 file.  No audio was altered otherwise.  Nothing has been released commercially. 

The filler is substantial, adding up to the first half (52 minutes) of the Detroit show.  But I don't see any LB number consistent with the timings presented.  There was no source data reported as well.
