A couple anecdotes from this show:

* It was part of Bucknell's Sequicentennial celebration, so it was a black-tie affair. Many of the students were dressed in gowns & tuxedos, while most everyone else had wore suit & ties. There were appetizers & an open bar & tables to sit at.

* Lots of drunken Bucknell students, many thanks to the open bar. Chanting for "Hurricane" began at some point & continues sporadically for the rest of the night.

* When Aimee Mann started performing, the floor was empty. We casually went up and got spaces right up front. The floor slowly started filling up during her performance. Got very crowded as Dylan started. A drunk girl worked her way up to the front row in between me and my friend. She wouldn't stop talking to me throughout the night. I started to get upset with her, enough so that Tony could obviously tell & started laughing about it. I shared a smile with him & then they asked me to come up on stage & sing on "Masters of War". Well, that last part didn't happen. But the drunk girl did ask that I take her to Erie, PA for the 10 May 1996 show, but I politely declined. Oh the fun we could have had. ;-)
