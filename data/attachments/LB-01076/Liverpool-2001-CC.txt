      Review: 
      Another great recording. Some of the tunes seems to be slightly off 
      tonight. Not bad, but Bob didn't seem to be in as good a mood as some of 
      the other shows along the tour. However, most of the acoustic numbers are 
      stunners. Visions Of Johanna and Boots Of Spanish Leather are powerful, 
      and this Desolation Row is worth the price of the collection. This is the 
      first set of the second wave of releases of the 2001 CC series. What a 
      beautiful package they present this year.

      Info: 
      Crystal Cat has unleashed the new kitty of CDs for the 2001 tour. The 
      packages are every bit as stunning as the 2000 crop. The full color, four 
      page booklet provides a wealth of venue photos and info. The discs are the 
      standard Crystal Cat logo CDs with various color motifs. The back photo, 
      and overall aesthetics remain similar. What has changed dramatically is 
      the front cover. Gone is the bland looking ticket stub of the previous 
      year; to be replaced with a sensual cartoon pinup girl of years past. With 
      the addition of the new 2001 material from the incredible  "Love and 
      Theft" album, the pinup girl image just seems to fit with the music of a 
      simpler, more innocent time. The quality of the recording is, needles to 
      say, breathtaking. With the February 2001 releases come incredible full 
      color photo tray liners as well. This release pictures the King's Dock 
      arena.
