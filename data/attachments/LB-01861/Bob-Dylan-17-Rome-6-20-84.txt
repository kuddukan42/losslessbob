Well we have gone from the highest fidelity show yesterday to the lowest one here.  It's 
really too bad to because the band is just incredible here.  I know nothing of this venue 
but the acoustics from this recording indicate an enclosed arena of some sort.  Again on 
this recording Mick Taylor is in the background, making the keyboard more prominent 
then we are use to hearing.  Taylor's solo on "I and I" is the best heard so far and the 
natural acoustic reverb from the building really adds to the hauntingly beautiful mood of 
the song here.  Also give listen to Taylor's solo on "Imagination."  It's simply mind-
boggeling.  Dylan is in fantastic voice and humor.  At one point near the end of the show 
he responds to an audience request with "I played that two hours ago."

THERE ARE SEVERAL PROBLEMS WITH THIS RECORDING!!!!!  There is 
diginoise at the end of disc 1.  Also, starting with "To Ramona" there are skips that may 
have been from the orginal tapes (this does not seem like a copying problem) making the 
lasts songs on disc 2 fragmented.  This is really quit annoying until you realize that 
Santana is going to be clip off, lol.
