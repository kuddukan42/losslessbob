Excerpt from "Performing Artist, 1986*1990 & beyond" by Paul Williams, p. 215 

In the summer 1989 Telegraph, John Bauldie quotes his friend Felix Canares, who said 
he had "a strange feeling all through the Stockholm show [the second 1989 concert], 
like anything could happen.  I was really worried that he wouldn't survive the show 
in a way - that he'd do something really bizarre.  He seemed to be so confused, 
started this really bad version of "Times Are A-Changing'", and his harmonica playing 
was terrible, really embarrasing.  He mixed up everything, started several songs in 
the wrong places...His singing was very low, rough voice, he wasn't even singing into 
the mike...He was really drunk, or influenced by drugs."
