Wembley Stadium
London, England
July 7, 1984

AUD + SBD recording

This is basically same recording as LB-4061, as uploaded by Fendert on May 9, 2005. It was banned and reuploaded with the offending tracks removed to satisfy dime policy. Honestly, I must confess it was just a lossy copy, in addition, nothing above 16k, so it should be discarded, but I wanted to preserve this Fendert version as complete as possible for documentary reasons and because I thought there was no other copy of this particular version, which indeed might sound much harsher and hollower than LB-4061, but clearer to my ears. I was convinced that this one turned out to be different to other versions of this concert Fendert shared and the subsequent reseeds ever posted. So my interest in the reconstruction and preservation of this piece, even if made out of tracks taken from different sources, like a lossy version of LB-4061, the soundboard of "Real Live" album and LB-4061 itself to fill the gaps. 1) "Real Live" CD to replace the offending tracks, but for d2t06

Bob Dylan & Mick Taylor
London
7-7-84

Audience Recording

**  MICK TAYLOR TORRENT WARS **


This is the next entry in the Dylan & Taylor STG remembrance concert series, lol.  This is the second to the last show of the 1984 tour.  This show, the Newcastle show (previously seeded), and the Slane Castle show (the next seed), were all recorded and eventually the material from these three shows became the album Real Live.  Most of that album originated on this night in London; a great show.  What is particularly interesting here is that you get to hear what they over-dubbed and fixed in the studio for the album Real Live, which as you will hear is absolutely nothing!  As weve come to expect from this group at this point in the tour the singing and playing is phenomenal.  Dylan pushes his vocal a little too much on a few songs but thats understandable with the end of the tour insight, the live recording and the special guest in great abundance at this show.  Towards the end of the show we get Santana again but luckily we also get Eric Clapton, Chrissie Hynde  which come on during Leopard Skin Pillbox Hat and Van Morrison comes on for Its All Over Now, Baby Blue.  The all-star jam portion is not great but still fun.  Great is Simple Twist of Fate with Mick Taylor transcending even himself.  Enough is Enough is not one of my favorite songs but even still Taylor makes it interesting, adding texture and style at every possible moment.  Of course I & I and License to Kill is great but then so are Every Grain of Sand and most of the others.  This well may be the pinnacle of the tour.  Enjoy!
