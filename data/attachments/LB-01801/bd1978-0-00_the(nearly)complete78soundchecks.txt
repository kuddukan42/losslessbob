Bob Dylan
The (Nearly) Complete 78 Soundchecks
Soundchecks before concerts
1978-00-00

Musicians:  Bob Dylan (vocals & guitar), Billy Cross (lead guitar), Alan Pasqua (keyboards), Steven Soles (rhythm guitar, backup vocals), David Mansfield (violin & mandolin), Steve Douglas (horns), Jerry Scheff (bass), Bobbye Hall (percussion), Ian Wallace (drums). Helena Springs, Jo Ann Harris, Carolyn Dennis (background vocals).

Source: Aud
Lineage: CD-Rs > EAC (secure mode) > CoolEdit Pro > CD Wave > FLAC Frontend > .flac (level 5)
Taped by: ?
Transferred by: ?
Sound Quality: passable to god-awful (I have posted an .mp3 with brief excerpts from each date so that you may judge whether this set is for you or not.  Just follow the thread down a few posts.)
Sound Processing/Editing: minor noise reduction, treble boost, bass cut & normalizing on most tracks; 09-17-78 edited from two separate versions of the same source (the lesser quality version was speed corrected to match the better quality one); 07-05-78 has beginning & end cut on account of serious mic abuse by the taper.  While I myself processed these files, to my ears they bear traces of previous sound processing (particularly the Paris soundchecks), so I really don't want to take full credit, particularly when the sounds get a bit "watery."  Still, they sound a heck of a lot better than what I received.  My original intent in fact was to distribute them "as is," but in the course of listening them for errors I decided that the trade off between "virgin" files and processed files was a reasonable & worthwhile one.

File Size: 1,708 megs .flac 

Notes: These tracks are difficult to listen to at times (e.g., 07-04-78), but highly rewarding.  I originally received these as a 6 disk set, but have decided to segregate them according to date, assuming that many will probably burn a "best-of" disk or two, and keep the rest archived for that occasional listen.  I bounced all tracks off of Olof's files & listed discrepancies where I found them (11-02-78 is extremely peculiar; see below).  On account of his listings I assume that this set is incomplete, but not by much.  Hence the title.  For the most part I have not listed "flaws" since they are numerous.  Surely there are better versions of these recordings circulating out there in Dylan-land, so please consider these as an educational tool till better comes around.  Heck, maybe several hundred years down the road one of Dylan's great-great grandkids will unearth a soundboard or two from these dates ;-).
