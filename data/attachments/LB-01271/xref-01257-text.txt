Bob Dylan
Hammersmith Odeon
London, UK
February 8, 1990

Holy Grail Version

Got this one at Dime as part of a big pack of five shows from 1990. Was described as LB-1271 but md5 checksums I obtained from my downloaded fileset didn't really match those of the LB archive. In fact those are shnf while these were flac. There was a semi-gap on left channel d1t02 @ 4:11 which is not reported to be at LB-1271. So I decided not to care about identity of the fileset (flac fingerprints) and managed to repair the gap I found. I respected the flacs for disc 2 except for track 14 where I heard clicks clearly visible at the spectral view in its waveform that were easy to clean. Disc 1 had only the mentioned flaw, but once I had to modify a few samples from d1t2 had to align on sector boundaries all the rest, as well, so I guessed d1 flacs would change since then. Fortunately, as I carefully patched a small number of samples from one channel to another without editing anything at all, the resulted wav file had no sector boundaries error origined. Consequently there are only 3 flac files different now from my original download in this set, d1t02, because of the gap elimination, and last two tracks on d2 after editing the clicks on d2t14 and align on sector boundaries. Did also rename them all from Track  1 to Track  9, giving the following names instead: Track 01 to Track 09, on both discs. Thanks to Charlespoet, the original seeder whom I did grab it from, and kisseber who was actually supplier of the audio disks where this set comes from (as Charles did state).

Small semi-gap on left channel d1t02 @ 4:11.999 repaired by patching 13 samples from the opposite channel.
Clicks d2t14 @ 1:49.415, 2:52.043 & 2.54.909 were easily micro-patched, but just a fragment of a single sample in each one had to be eliminated to make it as smooth as it gets.
Light diginoise on d2t06 @ 0:42.250 & 0:47.730 will have to remain as it was, as I couldn't find a way to solve the problem without damaging the sound in the recording.

Lineage: Kisseber's low gen audio disks > Charlespoet > wav (Unknown extraction) > wav (cooledit: digipops repair) > Flac (unknown software)
         flacs on d1 > wav (Flac Frontend 1.1.3) > d1t02.wav (Adobe Audition 1.0 - gap removed) > flac (Flac Frontend 1.1.3 - align on SB / just d1 - level 8 - verify on) 

Filling first of my good buddy Valentin requests.

Luisbp51 seed at HC. December 2007

Enjoy!
