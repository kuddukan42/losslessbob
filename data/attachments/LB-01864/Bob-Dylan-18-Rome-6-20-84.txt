Wow great show, another bad recording (the last of the poor recordings, BTW) but 
not near as bad as yesterdays.  This one is stunning even through the flaws and 
poor fidelity.  First this is not speed corrected (sorry, not enough time) but 
Dylan comes out smoking on the vocals right off the bat on "Highway 61 
Revisited" and the band does too.  The snare drum on this whole recording sounds 
great.  Something to do with the placement of the mic and the venue acoustics.  
There are some skips on this one but the show is still very listenable plus we 
get a few bonuses on this one.  "Desolation Row" is played for the first time in 
10 years and it is the only performance of it this tour.   Also, "Enough is 
Enough" makes its debut here and becomes a staple of the tour.  Dylan on this 
night does a harmonica solo on "Simple Twist of Fate" and then there is the 
Santana section.


THERE ARE SEVERAL PROBLEMS WITH THIS RECORDING!!!!!  
Skips here and there and the original recording was over-driven when recorded so 
that there is an slight audable static during certain sections.

cool to release each show to STG on the date of the actual show plus 20 
years. So, here is the eighteenth show; only 9 more!!! We'll see if STG can keep 
up with the rest of the tour; I can!!!  So far so good!
