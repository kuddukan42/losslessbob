The Lakes of Pontchartrain
(Carrie Baber arranged by Paul Joseph Brady)

'Twas on a bright March morning I bid New Orleans adieu.
I took the train from Jackson, and my fortune to renew.
I cursed all foreign money, no credit could I gain.
Which set my heart with longing for the lakes of Pontchartrain.

I hopped on board of a railway car, it was in the morning sun.
I rode the rods 'til day began then I laid me down again.
All strangers were no friends to me 'til a dark girl towards me came.
I fell in love with a Creole girl on the lakes of Pontchartrain.

I said, "Me pretty Creole girl, my money here is no good.
And if it weren't for the alligators, I'd sleep out in the wood."
"You're welcome here kind stranger, our home is very plain.
But we never turned a stanger out, on the lakes of Pontchartrain."

She took me in to her mammy's house and treated me quite well.
The hair upon her shoulders in jet black ringlets fell.
To try and paint her beauty, I'm sure 't would be in vain,
So handsome was my Creole girl, on the lakes of Pontchartrain.

I asked her if she would marry me, she said, "That never could be."
For she had got a lover, and he was far at sea.
She said that she be true to him, and true she would remain,
'Til he returned to his Creole girl, on the lakes of Pontchartrain.

It's fare thee well my Creole girl, I never will see you no more.
But I'll never forget your kindness in the cottage by the shore.
And at each social gathering, a flowin' glass I'll drain.
I'll drink a health to my Creole girl on the lakes of Pontchartrain.

-- Bob Dylan version - Madrid 1989-06-15
