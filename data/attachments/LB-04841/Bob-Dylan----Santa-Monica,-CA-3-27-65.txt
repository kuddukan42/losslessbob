The legend behind this 1965 audience recording is that
two girls smuggled a reel-to-reel tape recorder into
the show.  Listening to this is like watching a documentary
film: it does capture the moment.  Our heroines turned off
the recorder at times, probably to save battery power,
and some tracks are incomplete.  "With God On Our Side"
ends abruptly; "It Ain't Me Babe" is also cut short.  Some
songs may just be missing entirely (Hattie Carroll).
At times the girls can be heard, but they're not that
intrusive; mostly they seem to be worried about whether
the recorder is working.  It was, and the tape isn't
all that bad, considering the circumstances.
This may not be a pristine document like today's audience
recordings, but it does put you in the room at a gig
on Dylan's last entirely solo American tour.
