=== README.txt
===================
===================
===================
This README, together with the RTR "Songs of the Underground" guide and vine info file "rtr 76.txt.txt", is on every disc.  They are exactly the same on every disc.

This collection of 5 DVDs consists of the summer 2006 LK vine of 1976 Rolling Thunder shows and rehearsals.  It is exactly as I received it, with folders arranged on DVDs in the original way, with the following exceptions.

Any "Notes" files were on the original.  The Wolfgang's vault files were originally lossy, converted to Flac.

(1) I renamed the top folders, so that they are now arranged almost chronologically.  In every top folder, there is a file named "original_files.txt" that has the original name of the folder, as well as a list of all files that came on the vine, along with their sizes and md5 signatures.  The original folder names are also listed in "rtr 76.txt.txt".

(2) In every music folder, I created files data-sl.txt and data-ffp.txt that give, respectively, information from shntool (shntool len *.flac) and the flac fingerprints of all flac files.

(3) In every top folder, I added a link to Olof's online guide and a PDF file that contains Olof's information for the given show.  The Olof links may not be right for some of the rehearsals.

(4) At the top level of every DVD, I created an md5 file that can be used to verify all of the files on that DVD.

BIG EXCEPTION: On DVD 1, the vine included a folder "Rehearsals April 1976 (Alternate)".  I had downloaded these rehearsals from dime in October 2005 - LB 3295.  I concluded that the LK files were the same source (although the md5 signatures were not identical), so I didn't keep this folder from the LK vine.  I've included the LB 3295 version on DVD 1.

This vine consists of all the 1976 recordings that I'm aware of (66 CDs in total). Many are sourced from 'masters' and only 3 are from bootlegs (Un1976titled, Discover Broken & Hold the Fort.. (reissue)).These 3 bootlegs are particually good quality and complete, so I've used them.

I've added the other bits and pieces I have ( Ride 'em Jewboy - 25 Jan, Spanish is the Loving Tongue - 11 May and Sold American - 23 May) for completeness.

I've NOT included ANY of the (aud / PA) compilations: (22 April, 11 May, 16 May, 18 May & 23 May), but instead included BOTH the audience tape and the PA tape.

In general the tapes from 1976 are poorer quality than 1975 and almost No alternative recordings have emerged over the years, so this is a good as it gets, and all there is.

All shows are on DVD in FLAC format. 

No Artwork is included but I've attached set-lists & notes for each show.
A slightly updated 'Songs of the Underground' - with minor corrections to some Guam song titles is included.
Vault Disks 1-3 which include some new RTR tracks is included.
