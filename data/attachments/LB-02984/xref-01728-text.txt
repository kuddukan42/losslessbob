Bob Dylan And His Band

1994-07-10 Cologne, Germany (Tanzbrunnen)


From my old DAT (received in trade 1995-03-xx).  Based on various conversational snippets, this seems to be from the same source tape as LB-2984.  I always thought this was a digital transfer from a digital master, but the LB listings all refer to hiss (generally an analog thing).  The only other version of this tape I have is the "bittorrent from 01/16" mentioned in the LB-2984 entry -- I have not, for example, heard the "Love Minus Zero" bootleg version of this tape -- but comparing my DAT (this) to the 01/16 bittorrent, this has substantially less hiss.  (I compared a quiet song, Love Minus Zero, and the hiss is very noticeable over the beginning of the song on the 01/16 bittorrent version, but I hear literally zero hiss over the beginning of the song on this version.)  Sometimes I think I can hear some hiss on mine during the intervals between songs, but other times I think that might just be crowd rumble.  (I'm still personally inclined to believe that this may be a direct transfer from a digital master.)  One more thing:  the 01/16 bittorrent speculated (with some evidence) that this tape might have originated as a DSBD ...  It's an excellent audience tape, but there are moments when you can clearly hear the taper or some of his nearby sectionmates talking in between songs (in German, I think) -- so definitely not a soundboard.  But at this audio level who cares??

TRANSFER DATE: 2016-05-07

WHAT I DID: DAT > WAV > Sound Forge (broke intro tracks, fade in 1, fade out 14, removed 1:41 of encore interval audience noise after 12) > FLAC.

WHAT I DID NOT DO: resample the files (they remain @ 48 khz, just as on the original DAT, so you will have to convert them yourself in order to burn to CD).

All thanks to the original taper and to the person who sent me the DAT (who may or may not be the same person)!
